The Horses of Mausolus

In the Ancient Greek section of the museum you will find a horse's head. It belongs to a horse which use to stand (with three friends) atop the Mausoleum of Halikarnassos, the tomb of the tyrant Mausolus

The horse stands quietly with its head held down and turned to its left. It conveys a sense of great energy and vitality shown by the bulging muscles, flaring nostrils, open mouth, prominent veins and bulbous right eye.
